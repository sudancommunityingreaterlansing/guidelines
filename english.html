Phase 1: Foundation & Planning

Month 1-2

Community Needs Assessment

Key Questions for Sudanese Community:

  • What are the biggest challenges facing Sudanese families in Lansing?
  • What cultural traditions are most important to preserve?
  • How many Sudanese students are at MSU? What are their needs?
  • What regional/tribal diversity exists within our community?
  • What resources are currently available vs. what's needed?

Mission Statement Development

Recommended Template:

"The Sudanese Community of Greater Lansing (SCGL) exists to preserve and promote Sudanese cultural heritage, foster unity among Sudanese diaspora, support the successful integration of new arrivals, and contribute positively to the broader Lansing community through education, advocacy, and mutual support."

Lansing-Specific Advantages

  • MSU Partnership: African Studies Center, Office of Cultural and Academic Transitions
  • Capital City Resources: State government connections, funding opportunities
  • Existing Networks: Refugee Development Center, interfaith communities
  • Cultural Hub: Growing African diaspora community in Greater Lansing

Phase 2: Legal Formation (Michigan)

Step-by-Step Process

Month 2

Choose and Reserve Name

  • Search Michigan LARA Corporation Database
  • Ensure name includes "Sudanese" for cultural identity
  • Consider: "Sudanese Community in Greater Lansing"
  • Check website domain availability

Draft Bylaws - Cultural Considerations

Key Provisions for Sudanese Context:

  • Define "Sudanese community" inclusively (all regions, tribes)
  • Membership tiers with affordable options for students/new arrivals
  • Voting rights that respect both elders and youth
  • Cultural committees: Language, Youth, Women's, Newcomer Support
  • Conflict resolution process honoring traditional mediation

File Articles of Incorporation

Form 102 with Michigan LARA
Filing Fee: $20
Processing Time: 7-10 business days
Link: Form 502
    This document is to be used pursuant to the provisions of Act 162, P.A. of 1982, by one or more persons for the purpose of forming a domestic nonprofit corporation. Can be completed online. The purpose for which the corporation is formed must be included. A domestic nonprofit corporation may be formed on either a membership or directorship basis. A membership corporation entitles the members to vote in determining corporate action A post office box may not be designated as the address of the registered office. The address(es) should include a street number and name (or other designation), city and state. This document may be completed and submitted online at www.michigan.gov/corpfileonline.

Purpose Clause Example:

"To promote Sudanese cultural heritage through educational programs, festivals, and language preservation; to provide charitable assistance to Sudanese immigrants and refugees in integration; to foster mutual support and community development among Sudanese-Americans in the Lansing region..."

Phase 3: Dual Board Formation

Two-Tier Governance Model

Board of Directors (Required) Advisory Board (Recommended)
Legal governance authority Advisory role only
Fiduciary responsibility No legal liability
Manages operations & finances Provides wisdom & connections
7-9 members, elected 7-11 members, appointed
2-year terms, term limits 2-year terms, renewable

Board of Directors - Recommended Composition

Essential Positions for Sudanese Community: (suggested)

  • President: Respected leader, bilingual preferred
  • Vice President: MSU connection if possible
  • Secretary: Bilingual, organized
  • Treasurer: Financial literacy required
  • Cultural Director: Elder or tradition-keeper
  • Youth Director: 18-35 years old
  • Women's Representative: Addresses women's specific needs
  • Newcomer Advocate: Recently settled refugee
    • The board now has 8 position Plan to add 2 more positions

Advisory Board Formation Process

Month 3

Recruitment Strategy

Target 3 Categories:

  • Total number (6-9): Can be invited to assume the roles
  • Cultural Elders (2-3): Community founders, wisdom keepers
  • Institutional Bridges (2-4): MSU professor, city official, interfaith leader
  • Professional Experts (2-4): Lawyer, doctor, educator, business owner

Cultural Recruitment Protocol

How to invite/approach the advisory board memebers:

  • For elders: Personal visit with small gift (dates, tea)
  • Use existing relationships and family connections
  • Frame as: "We need your wisdom to guide our community"
  • Emphasize modest time commitment: 4 meetings/year

Phase 4: Internal Bylaws & Operational Structure

Sudanese Community Association of Greater Lansing

Internal Regulations (2026-2027)

Table of Contents:

  1. Discipline and Accountability
  2. Meetings
  3. Expenditure Items and Percentages
  4. Annual Programs
  5. Annual Plan
  6. Monitoring and Evaluation

1. Meetings

  1. The Executive Committee shall hold regular meetings at least twice per month.
  2. A meeting is legal with the attendance of a simple majority of members.
  3. Decisions are made by majority vote. In case of a tie, the President's side prevails.
  4. Emergency meetings may be held when necessary.

2. Discipline and Accountability

  1. Committee members must attend meetings and participate actively.
  2. In case of repeated absence without acceptable excuse, the committee may take appropriate measures.
  3. All members must adhere to the Code of Conduct and Ethics.

3. Expenditure Items and Percentages

  1. In case of death (community support)
  2. Public occasions (hall rentals, etc.)
  3. Administrative supplies
  4. Social assistance

4. Annual Programs

  1. Independence Day Celebration
  2. Ramadan Iftar
  3. Eid al-Adha Celebration
  4. Community Social Day (Summer trip with programs including graduate recognition)
  5. Christmas Celebration

5. Annual Plan

The annual plan can be divided into three periods:

  1. Short-term Plan (January-March):
    • Writing the Constitution
    • Writing Internal Regulations
    • Collecting data from community contributors
    • Registering the community organization
    • Obtaining EIN Number
    • Opening bank account for the community
    • Renting a location for the community
    • Establishing system for monthly/annual contributions
  2. Medium-term Plan (April-August):
    • Renting a permanent headquarters for the community
    • Providing necessary equipment for headquarters (chairs, tables, carpets, etc.)
    • Preparing semi-annual financial report
  3. Long-term Plan (12 months):
    • [To be developed based on community needs assessment]

6. Monitoring and Evaluation

  • Report every three months
  • Review of goals
  • Course correction
  • Presenting results to members

How to Integrate These Bylaws with Legal Requirements

Key Integration Points:

  • Legal vs. Cultural: The "Executive Committee" = Board of Directors legally
  • Financial Transparency: Expenditure items must align with IRS public benefit requirements
  • Meeting Requirements: Twice monthly is excellent for community engagement
  • Annual Programs: Perfect for 501(c)(3) application - shows cultural and educational activities
  • Evaluation System: Quarterly reporting aligns with good nonprofit governance

Phase 5: IRS Tax Exemption

Choosing Your Application Path

Form 1023-EZ Form 1023 (Long Form)
Eligibility: Projected receipts < $50,000 for 3 years Eligibility: Any organization size
Fee: $275 Fee: $600
Processing: 2-4 weeks Processing: 3-12 months
Length: 3 pages Length: 28 pages + schedules
Recommended for: Most community startups Needed if: Large budget or complex activities

Using Your Annual Programs in the Application

Cultural & Educational Programs (501(c)(3) Qualified)

  • Independence Day Celebration: Cultural education for broader community
  • Ramadan Iftar: Interfaith understanding and cultural exchange
  • Community Social Day: Social cohesion and youth development
  • Graduate Recognition: Educational achievement celebration

Charitable Programs (501(c)(3) Qualified)

  • Social Assistance: Direct aid to community members in need
  • Death Support: Charitable relief during bereavement
  • Newcomer Support: Assistance with integration
Important: Frame all programs as providing public benefit.
Example: "Our Ramadan Iftar is open to all community members to promote interfaith understanding."

Phase 6: Michigan State Compliance

Required State Registrations

After IRS Approval

1. Michigan Tax Exemption

  • Form 3372: Application for Michigan Tax Exemption
  • Submit to Michigan Department of Treasury
  • Exempts from Michigan income tax
  • Sales/Use Tax Exemption for nonprofit purchases
  • All corporations are required to continuously maintain a registered office and resident agent in Michigan.

2. Charitable Registration

  • Form CTS: Initial Charitable Organization Registration - CTS - 01 Initial Solicitation Form: (online fillable form)
  • Required BEFORE soliciting donations
  • File with Michigan Attorney General's Charitable Trust Section
  • Annual Filing: annual report (Form 519) (due 6 months after fiscal year ends)
  • Here is more info form
  • Due: On or before October 1 of each year beginning the year after incorporation
  • Fee: $20.00. Enclose check or money order payable to the “State of Michigan”
  • Forms: A preprinted annual report, CSCL/CD-2000 is mailed to the corporation’s resident agent at the registered office approximately three months prior to the due date.
  • Filing: The report can be filed online at www.michigan.gov/corpfileonline

3. Local Lansing Requirements

  • Check with Lansing City Clerk for business license requirements
  • Ensure meeting space complies with zoning regulations
  • Apply for special event permits for festivals
  • Consider insurance: General liability, D&O coverage

  • Create emergency support fund
